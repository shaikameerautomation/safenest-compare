Section 10 — Things to Know

A few things that deserve your attention

These are not reasons to walk away. They are specific items that every informed buyer should understand and verify before signing. Your SafeNest advisor will guide you through each one at no cost.

Bank construction mortgage is active on the land
Prestige Group has taken a construction loan from Axis Bank against this project's land. This is completely standard — almost every RERA-registered project carries one. It does not affect your purchase, but it requires one extra step: this mortgage must be officially released before your flat is registered in your name. This is the developer's responsibility to arrange.
What to do: Ask your independent lawyer to confirm the mortgage release clause is clearly written in your Agreement of Sale before you sign. This takes a lawyer approximately 30 minutes to verify.
Possession is December 2026 — plan a buffer
The project is in Finishing Stage and the December 2026 timeline appears achievable based on our site visit. Finishing work — common areas, landscaping, OC documentation — can sometimes run 2–3 months longer. We are not flagging this as a concern; we are asking you to factor it into your planning.
What to do: Plan your move-in arrangements with a buffer of 3 months beyond December 2026. Ask the developer for a written construction milestone schedule at the time of booking.
Section 11 — Before You Sign

Your step-by-step checklist

Before Booking
  • 1
    Visit the site. Walk your floor. Seeing the ceiling height and the construction quality in person tells you more than any report.
  • 2
    Verify RERA number P02400007891 yourself at rera.telangana.gov.in — takes 2 minutes.
  • 3
    Get your home loan pre-approved by SBI, HDFC, or ICICI before you book. They have already approved this project — this step is fast.
Before Signing the Agreement of Sale
  • 4
    Hire an independent property lawyer (not the developer's lawyer) to review the AOS. Specifically: check the mortgage release clause, delay penalty, and OC timeline. Expect to pay ₹5,000–10,000.
  • 5
    Confirm your exact flat number, floor, facing, and car park slot in writing on the AOS letterhead. Verbal agreements do not hold legally.
Before Registration
  • 6
    Confirm the Occupancy Certificate has been issued or applied for before proceeding with registration. Without OC, the flat is technically not legally occupiable.
  • 7
    Confirm the Axis Bank construction mortgage has been officially released (NOC from Axis Bank). Your lawyer verifies this. Must be done before your registration appointment.
