DIPLOMATIC LANGUAGE RULES (always apply):
  ✓ "One aspect to review before proceeding" not "Red flag"
  ✓ "Deserves your attention" not "Warning"
  ✓ "An item to verify" not "A problem"
  ✓ "Standard practice" when something is normal but worth noting
  ✓ "We recommend verifying" not "You must check"
  ✗ Never say: dangerous, alarming, risky, problem, failure
  ✗ Never omit a genuine risk — disclosure is mandatory
  ✗ Never soften to the point of obscuring what the risk is

  "SafeNest did not identify any significant concerns requiring
   your attention for this project at the time of this report."
